About Me

I’m Nathan Brown-Bennett, a cybersecurity researcher, software developer, and MSc student in Network and Information Security at Kingston University London. I hold a BSc in Cyber Security & Digital Forensics and have experience building technical tools, educational platforms, and research-driven applications that explore the intersection of security, education, and accessibility.

My work focuses on designing solutions that are both technically robust and user-friendly. I’ve led and co-developed several software projects including:

  • Dynamic Flowchart for Device Provisioning
  • Cyclomatic Complexity Calculator
  • MyMark – Media fingerprinting & blockchain watermarking tool
  • BlockBash – Visual cybersecurity education platform
  • PicChat – Secure & Anonymouse Social Media that encrypts conversations and hides them steganographically
  • HackerGo – Gamified cybersecurity adventure app

I’ve delivered workshops, taught scripting, led student clubs, and collaborated with mentors and researchers. I’m passionate about ethical hacking, AI security, and building tech for social impact.
